# OPS-SOP-315 — Incident Command Handoff

**Owner:** Operations Governance  
**Revision:** 1.6  
**Classification:** Synthetic training material — unclassified

## 1. Purpose

Transfer incident command between watch teams without losing decisions, evidence, ownership, or the operational picture.

## 2. Handoff package

The outgoing incident commander prepares the current impact, start time, severity, affected services, known-good facts, open hypotheses, completed actions, action results, current risks, communications cadence, external contacts, and next three planned actions.

## 3. Verbal handoff

1. Conduct the handoff on the incident bridge with both commanders present.
2. State the current customer and mission impact first.
3. Separate confirmed facts from hypotheses.
4. Review every active workstream, owner, blocker, and next checkpoint.
5. Identify any dangerous or irreversible action awaiting approval.
6. The incoming commander repeats back severity, priorities, and the next decision point.

## 4. Transfer of command

The incident scribe records the exact transfer time. The incoming commander explicitly accepts command and announces the change to all participants. Ownership does not transfer silently when a shift ends.

## 5. Post-handoff check

Within fifteen minutes, the incoming commander verifies monitoring access, contact paths, action owners, and the next stakeholder update time.
